# Outreach message patterns

All patterns: under 100 words, one ask, written like a person. Personalize the [bracketed] slots from the connections data and career profile — never send a template with generic filler.

## 1. Reconnect-then-ask (dormant 1st-degree contact)

> Hi [name] — it's been a while since [shared context: worked together at X / connected around Y]. I've been [one line: current situation]. I'm exploring [target role] opportunities and saw you're at [company]. Would you be open to a quick chat about what it's like there? Happy to keep it to 15 minutes.

Use when: connected >1 year ago, real shared history exists.

## 2. Direct ask (warm, recent, or strong contact)

> Hi [name] — I'm targeting [role] roles and [company] is at the top of my list, partly because [specific, true reason]. I saw [posting/team]. Could I ask you two quick questions about the team? And if it seems like a fit, would you be comfortable referring me?

Use when: relationship is warm enough that hedging would feel odd. The referral ask is explicit — most companies pay referral bonuses; this is a favor that costs them little.

## 3. Intro request (bridge contact → someone inside)

> Hi [name] — quick favor. I'm pursuing [role] at [company], and I noticed you [reason they'd know someone: used to work there / work closely with them]. Is there someone on the [team] side you'd feel comfortable introducing me to? Happy to send a short blurb you can forward.

Always offer the forwardable blurb — it drops the bridge's effort to near zero. Draft the blurb too (3 sentences, third person, one metric).

## 4. Recruiter note

> Hi [name] — I'm a [positioning line from career profile] exploring [target role] roles in [domain]. I saw you recruit for [space]. My background: [one metric-backed line]. Worth a conversation if you're working anything that fits — resume attached.

Use when: contact is an agency or in-house recruiter. Recruiters want signal fast; skip all warm-up.

## Anti-patterns (never)

- "I hope this message finds you well"
- Asking for "any opportunities" (make the target role specific)
- Fake flattery about their "impressive journey"
- More than one ask per message
- Mentioning the full job-search context to a contact at the user's current employer if the search is confidential
